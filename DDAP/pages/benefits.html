If you, or someone you care about is having a problem with addiction there are several basic ways to get help:

There is a county drug and alcohol office that you can call that can help walk you through your options for getting help. (In some areas, there is one office that serves one or more counties.) These offices focus on recovery and addiction treatment resources. You can locate the phone number for this office in the blue pages of your local phone book under “county offices” or by searching for the office in the county where you live on the following website:
 

YES
If you have health insurance, including medical assistance, there should be a phone number on your insurance card that you can call to find out how to access services. The card may say, “For Behavioral Health Services” or “For Drug and Alcohol Services” or “For Substance Abuse Services” call the number provided.

NO
If you do not have health insurance and are in financial need, please visit the Care Provider page to search for local funding organizations. You may also visit the supplied website to help you get the funding for the services that are necessary.

If you still need help finding out how to get help, you can call the Pennsylvania Department of Drug and Alcohol Programs at 717-783-8200.
